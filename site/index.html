Introduction

I am a fifth-year Ph.D. candidate with a concentration in developmental science at the Psychology and Brain Sciences Department, Boston University, USA. I work on cognitive development in 2-12-year children. My Ph.D. thesis is on the role of topological properties in the development of perception, attention, working memory, and object categorization. In my future research, I plan to work on how adversity impact the brain and cognitive development in international populations (primarily in developing countries). On another side of the same coin, I would like to investigate how children in developing countries conceptualize inequality, status hierarchies, blatant discrimination, and dehumanization.
